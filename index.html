Overview

This portfolio artifact encapsulates an interactive journey into three critical machine learning (ML) challenges — missing data, noisy data, and class imbalance — tackled through a real-time collaboration with an AI chatbot. Each scenario presented increasing complexity, allowing me to progressively enhance my data handling skills and decision-making frameworks. The experience was grounded in hands-on coding practices and supported by algorithmic reasoning.

This activity was more than a technical exercise—it was a simulation of critical thinking, decision-making, and AI collaboration. The AI chatbot acted as a peer, guiding me through different layers of preprocessing strategy based on real-world examples. It was my first experience with reflective AI dialogue that emphasized reasoning, not just results. This shaped my awareness of what it means to be both a data scientist and a responsible designer.

In addition to the technical scenarios, I explored leadership in AI through a personal value statement and strategies to address bias in educational settings. My goal is to blend ethical AI development with practical application to ensure inclusive and fair outcomes. This aligns with my broader vision of responsible innovation in both the tech and education sectors.

Process

Step 1: Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA) to detect missing values and patterns.
Step 2: Applied median imputation and predictive modeling to restore missing data.
Step 3: Cleaned noisy datasets using moving averages, smoothing filters, and anomaly filtering.
Step 4: Addressed class imbalance using SMOTE, ensemble techniques, and weighted classifiers.
Step 5: Reflected on AI ethics and wrote a leadership framework addressing algorithmic bias in education.
Tools Used: Python, pandas, scikit-learn, matplotlib, seaborn, imbalanced-learn.

Chatbot-Guided ML Scenarios

Scenario 1 – Missing Data

Used strategies like MCAR/MAR detection, median imputation, regression-based imputation, and missingness flagging. The chatbot supported with both techniques and rationale for method selection.

Scenario 2 – Noisy Sensor Data

Preprocessing included visualization, IQR-based outlier filtering, rolling mean smoothing, and signal denoising using exponential methods.

Scenario 3 – Class Imbalance

Addressed imbalance with SMOTE, class-weighted classifiers (Random Forest), AUC-ROC and F1 metrics, and anomaly detection options like Isolation Forest and autoencoders.
