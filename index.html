Overview

This artifact demonstrates my application of K-Means clustering combined with experiential learning through chatbot-led AI/ML scenarios and ethical leadership reflection activities. I practiced resolving real-world data issues through guided case studies covering missing data, noisy signals, and class imbalance. Each scenario pushed me to design practical, evidence-based preprocessing pipelines and validated my decision-making process.

Additionally, I developed a leadership framework rooted in integrity and inclusion. In my reflection "Navigating Human Bias as a Leader in AI and Education," I explored how ethical considerations in model design can be systematically integrated through data audits, inclusive design, and stakeholder feedback loops. These ideas now influence how I structure my AI problem-solving from both technical and ethical standpoints.

This comprehensive experience helped align my technical skill development with reflective thinking—establishing the foundations of my identity as an AI practitioner committed to both innovation and fairness.

Process

Step 1: Loaded dataset and handled missing values using median imputation.
Step 2: Detected noisy sensor readings and smoothed them using rolling average.
Step 3: Used Elbow and Silhouette methods to determine optimal k for K-Means.
Step 4: Applied SMOTE for class imbalance in separate classification practice.
Step 5: Applied K-Means clustering and visualized segments for business insight.

Key Takeaways

  • Chatbot activities helped simulate real decision-making in ML workflows.

  • Elbow and Silhouette methods provide strong justifications for clustering parameter choices.
  • AI ethics and bias mitigation are continuous processes requiring leadership and accountability.
  • Communication of results through visualization improves stakeholder trust and understanding.

Reflection

This portfolio piece gave me hands-on practice with advanced clustering workflows and deeper insight into real-world data complexity. The scenarios enhanced my awareness of how data imperfections impact model outcomes and helped me apply techniques like SMOTE, median imputation, and smoothing with confidence. It also showed me how critical the "pre" in preprocessing really is.

Through the ethical leadership component, I learned that bias in AI is not always about bad intentions—often it stems from omission, lack of voice, or misaligned goals. As I advance in this field, I will carry a dual commitment: to build high-performing models, and to ensure that the human stories behind data are acknowledged, respected, and safeguarded.
